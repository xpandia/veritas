# VERITAS — Video Storyboard
### 60-90 Second Submission Video
#### GenLayer Testnet Bradbury Hackathon

---

## TONE & STYLE

- **Visual language:** Dark, minimal, authoritative. Think Apple keynote meets legal tech.
- **Color palette:** Charcoal (#1a1a2e) background, electric blue (#0066ff) accents, white text.
- **Typography:** Inter. Clean. Let the words breathe.
- **Music:** Low, building electronic pulse. No lyrics. Tension that resolves.
- **Pacing:** Slow and deliberate for the problem. Fast and precise for the solution. Let the contrast do the work.
- **Voice:** Single narrator. Calm. Confident. Every sentence earns its place.

## PRODUCTION NOTES

### Screen Recordings Needed
1. **Dispute filing flow** — the full form, from empty to submitted (capture at 2x resolution, we'll zoom in post)
2. **Transaction confirmation** — the on-chain receipt on GenLayer testnet
3. **Verdict display** — the AI reasoning and final decision appearing
4. **Escrow release** — funds moving, wallet balance updating

### Animations Needed
1. **Network consensus visualization** — nodes independently processing, then converging (can be done in After Effects or Motion, or even CSS animation screen-captured)
2. **Split-screen comparison** — the 18-months-vs-5-minutes timeline race
3. **Logo assembly** — Veritas wordmark forming from network nodes

### Audio
- **Music:** Recommend sourcing from Artlist or Epidemic Sound. Search: "minimal tech," "cinematic tension resolve," "dark corporate." The track needs a clear shift at 0:25 (from tension to momentum).
- **Voiceover:** Record in a quiet room. Speak slowly. Let pauses do the work. The script is 180 words — at 150 WPM with pauses, it fits 75-80 seconds comfortably.

### Timing Budget
| Section | Duration | Cumulative |
